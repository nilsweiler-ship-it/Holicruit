# Partner target shortlist (research-based)

A starting shortlist of realistic partner targets that fit Holicruit's thesis,
with a "why them" hook for each. Training providers are named because there's a
well-mapped market; for employers I've given targeting *criteria* + example
profiles rather than cold-naming specific firms — pick from your own network
first, since a warm intro beats any list.

> Geography note: the app uses € pricing and multi-industry personas, so this
> skews European / multi-market. Adjust if your launch market differs.

---

## 1. Training & upskilling providers

These populate the **Growth Paths** marketplace — the fourth-party revenue line.
Holicruit's edge for them: gap-targeted placement, ranked on real outcomes
(completions, re-matches), not ad spend. Best first partners are ones proud of
their completion/outcome data, because our ranking rewards exactly that.

### Open lanes (a targeting opportunity, not a name)

Your personas span healthcare, sales, and finance, but the bootcamp market is
software-heavy. That means **healthcare / sales / finance training providers**
face little competition inside Growth Paths today — lead those conversations with
variant A4 and the "open lane" framing.

## 2. Employers & hiring managers (design partners)

Don't cold-blast a list — recruit 3–5 design partners, ideally warm intros.
Prioritize by which pain your product answers best:

- **High applicant volume** → variant B1. Profile: consumer brands, marketplaces,
  anywhere a single posting draws hundreds of applicants. Pain: can't read the pile fairly.
- **Fierce talent competition** → variant B2. Profile: funded startups / scale-ups
  hiring engineers. Pain: rejected candidates are future applicants and referrers.
- **Distributed / frontline / high-turnover** → variant B3. Profile: healthcare
  systems, retail/ops. Pain: résumé scans miss the human skills that predict success.

**Ideal design partner checklist:** hiring 3+ roles now · a named hiring
manager who'll actually use the pipeline weekly · cares about employer brand ·
small enough to move fast, real enough to give credible feedback.

---

## 3. Suggested sequencing

1. **Two employer design partners first** (from your network). Their live roles
   make the provider pitch concrete — "candidates with X gap are already here."
2. **Two or three providers next**, one per major gap type, leading with software.
3. Use early partner names (with permission) as proof in the next wave and in the
   investor deck.
